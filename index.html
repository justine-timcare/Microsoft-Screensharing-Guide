How to Share Your Screen
in Microsoft Teams

A step-by-step guide for the best experience

Know your options

3 ways to share your screen

Entire Screen

  • Shows everything on your monitor
  • Participants see your desktop, taskbar, and all open apps
  • Best when switching between multiple apps

Specific Window

  • Shares only one application window
  • Other apps and notifications stay private
  • Best for focused presentations

Browser Tab

  • Shares a single browser tab only
  • Other tabs and apps stay hidden
  • Best for showing a website or web app
Recommendation: Use "Window" sharing for most meetings -- it keeps your desktop private.

Step-by-step: start sharing

1

Open the Share Tray

Click the Share button (rectangle with arrow) in the meeting toolbar, or press Ctrl+Shift+E

2

Choose What to Share

Select Screen, Window, or Browser Tab from the options shown

3

Pick the Right Content

If you chose Window, click the specific app you want to share

4

Include Computer Sound (Optional)

Toggle "Include computer sound" ON if sharing a video or audio clip

Quick shortcut: Press Ctrl + Shift + E to open the share tray instantly.
Do it right

Best practices for screen sharing

Close personal apps first

Close email, messages, and personal browser tabs before sharing your entire screen

Include sound when needed

Toggle "Include computer sound" before sharing if you are playing a video or audio clip

Disable notifications

Turn on Do Not Disturb or Focus Assist in Windows to block pop-up notifications

Give control when asked

Click "Give control" in the sharing toolbar to let someone else navigate your screen

Remember: Participants can see everything on a shared screen, including personal notifications!

When you are done

Stopping and giving control

How to Stop Sharing

  • Click the Stop Sharing button in the toolbar at the top of your screen
  • Or press Ctrl + Shift + E again to toggle off
  • The red border around your screen will disappear

How to Give Control

  • Click "Give control" in the sharing toolbar
  • Select the participant's name from the list
  • They can now move your mouse and type
  • Click "Take back control" when done
Look for the red/yellow border around your screen -- that means you are still sharing.

Fix it fast

Troubleshooting common issues

Participants see a black screen

Close and reopen the share tray. Make sure the app is not minimized.

No sound when sharing a video

Toggle "Include computer sound" ON before you start sharing.

Share button is grayed out

The meeting organizer may have disabled sharing. Ask the host to enable it.

Screen sharing is laggy

Close unused apps. Share a specific window instead of the entire screen.

PowerPoint shows presenter view

Use "PowerPoint Live" from the share tray, or switch to single-monitor mode.
